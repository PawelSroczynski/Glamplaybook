## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 The Challenge

Enklava Yurts operates as a successful craft business building custom yurts. The ambition is to scale this into an international franchise operation. However, the current operational model is fundamentally **person-dependent** rather than **system-dependent**.

### 1.2 Core Diagnosis

> **Current State:** The business works because YOU do it.
> **Problem:** You cannot "copy and paste" yourself to another country.

The existing client flow (Jurta v3.0) represents a **craftsman's workflow**, not a **franchise-ready business system**. While effective for current operations, it lacks the documentation, standardization, quality controls, and technological infrastructure required for replication.

### 1.3 Key Findings

| Aspect | Current Score | Franchise-Ready Score |
|--------|---------------|----------------------|
| Process Clarity | 7/10 | Needs 9/10 |
| Completeness | 4/10 | Needs 9/10 |
| Scalability | 2/10 | Needs 8/10 |
| Franchise-Readiness | 2/10 | Needs 8/10 |

### 1.4 Primary Recommendation

Before pursuing international expansion, the business must prove its systems work **without the founder** - first in Poland, with 2-3 people trained from zero using documented systems only.

## 3. Critical System Gaps

### 3.1 Gap #1: Knowledge Layer (CRITICAL)

**What's Missing:**

| Element | Description | Franchise Impact |
|---------|-------------|------------------|
| SOPs (Standard Operating Procedures) | Step-by-step instructions for every process | New franchisee doesn't know HOW to do each step |
| Quality Checklists | Inspection criteria at each stage | No quality control = inconsistent output across locations |
| Document Templates | Contracts, proposals, invoices | Everyone does it differently = legal chaos |
| Technical Knowledge Base | Materials specs, construction methods, troubleshooting | How to sew a cover? What wood to use? What insulation? |

**Consequence:** If the founder disappears tomorrow, the business dies.

**Required Deliverables:**
- [ ] 15-20 Standard Operating Procedures (with video)
- [ ] Quality checklist per production stage
- [ ] Template library (contracts, proposals, invoices)
- [ ] Technical manual with specifications

## 6. Strategic Questions to Resolve

Before proceeding with franchise development, the following strategic decisions must be made:

### 6.1 Production Model

**Question:** Where will yurts be produced?

| Option | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|------|
| **Central (Poland exports)** | Quality control, expertise concentration | Shipping costs, lead times, customs |
| **Local (Franchisee produces)** | Lower logistics cost, faster delivery | Training intensive, QC challenge, equipment investment |
| **Hybrid (Components central, assembly local)** | Balance of control and flexibility | Complex coordination |

**Recommended Approach:** Start with Central, evolve to Hybrid as franchise matures.

### 6.2 Sales Model

**Question:** Who owns the customer relationship?

| Option | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|------|
| **HQ generates leads, franchisee closes** | Consistent brand, economies of scale | Franchisee dependency, lead quality disputes |
| **Franchisee generates and closes** | Local market knowledge, motivated franchisee | Inconsistent brand, variable quality |
| **Shared (HQ brand, franchisee local)** | Combined strengths | Complex attribution, potential conflicts |

**Recommended Approach:** Shared model with clear territory definitions and lead source tracking.

### 6.3 Assembly Model

**Question:** Who performs on-site assembly?

| Option | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|------|
| **Certified local installers** | Scalable, lower cost | Training investment, QC challenge |
| **HQ traveling team** | Consistent quality, expertise | High cost, scheduling constraints |
| **Customer self-assembly** | Lowest cost, scalable | Support burden, liability risk |
| **Hybrid (complex=HQ, simple=local)** | Flexibility | Complexity in decision criteria |

**Recommended Approach:** Certified local installer program with HQ supervision for first 3-5 assemblies per franchisee.

### 6.4 Customization Scope

**Question:** How much customization is allowed?

| Level | Description | Implication |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| **None** | Fixed models only | Easy to scale, limited market |
| **Low** | Standard models + predefined options | Balanced, recommended starting point |
| **Medium** | Modifications within parameters | Requires approval process |
| **High** | Full custom (founder involvement) | Premium pricing, limited scale |

**Recommended Approach:** Low customization standard, Medium available with HQ approval, High only direct from HQ.

### 6.5 Revenue Model

**Question:** How will HQ generate revenue from franchisees?

| Component | Options | Consideration |
|-----------|---------|---------------|
| **Initial Fee** | â‚¬20K-100K | Market-dependent, covers training |
| **Royalty** | 4-8% of revenue | Ongoing support funding |
| **Marketing Fund** | 1-2% of revenue | Central marketing activities |
| **Product Markup** | 10-30% on supplies | If HQ supplies materials |
| **Training Fees** | Per person/course | Ongoing certification |

**Recommended Approach:** Initial fee + Royalty + Marketing fund. Product markup optional based on production model.

### 6.6 Territory Structure

**Question:** How will territories be defined?

| Approach | Description | Consideration |
|----------|-------------|---------------|
| **Exclusive geographic** | Country/region exclusivity | Clear, but may limit growth |
| **Non-exclusive** | Multiple franchisees per area | Competition risk |
| **Performance-based** | Exclusivity tied to targets | Motivation, but complexity |
| **Master franchise** | Sub-franchising rights | For large markets |

**Recommended Approach:** Exclusive geographic for initial markets, Master franchise for large countries (Germany, UK, France).
